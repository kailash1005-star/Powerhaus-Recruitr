# Recruitr — Sales Pitch Deck (Germany)
### 5 slides · BCG-style · problem-first · vendor- & platform-neutral

> **How to use this doc:** Each slide below has (1) the **action title** (the one takeaway —
> BCG rule: titles are full sentences, not labels), (2) the **minimal on-slide content**,
> (3) a **layout/visual note**, and (4) the **spoken script** (~45–60 sec, executive tone).
> Keep the slides sparse. The words are spoken, not printed.

## Design system (apply to every slide)

**Colours (Vercel-minimal — use only these):**
| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Deep Navy | `#0B1B33` | Titles, dark section slides, key figures |
| Light Blue | `#3D9BFF` | **One** accent per slide — the single number/word that matters |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Dominant background; ~60% of every slide is empty space |
| Black | `#000000` | Body text |
| (Neutral grey) | `#6B7280` | Secondary captions only |

**BCG principles baked in:** one message per slide · answer-first (pyramid) · MECE problem
breakdown · data-forward · max ~15 words of body text per slide · exactly one accent colour
highlight per slide. **Typography:** Geist or Inter. Titles ~40px bold navy; body ~20px.
No clip-art, no gradients, no more than one chart per slide.

## Competitive read — Ceipal (the reference)

Ceipal positions as a **closed "Staffing OS"** — *"Stop Switching Systems… the entire talent
lifecycle runs through one system."* Strong vision, but the implicit ask is **rip-and-replace**.
Their AI runs an 8-agent lifecycle: JD Parser → Candidate Search → Rediscover Similarity →
Matching & Ranking → Email Personalization → Outreach → Scheduler → Submission.

**Recruitr's counter-position:** the same agentic lifecycle, but **neutral** — it runs *on the
ATS/CRM you already own*, and it is **built for the German compliance bar** Ceipal does not
foreground. We don't ask agencies to switch systems; we make their current stack hire faster.

**Recruitr agent lineup — be honest about Live vs Releasing:**
| Lifecycle agent | Recruitr status |
|---|---|
| JD Parser (skills/experience/location from JD) | **Live** |
| Candidate Search & Sourcing (scores/sources active candidates) | **Live** |
| Rediscover Similarity (re-mine your own database) | **Releasing** |
| Matching & Ranking (rank with explainable score) | **Releasing** |
| Email Personalization | **Releasing** |
| Outreach (send + capture responses) | **Releasing** |
| Scheduler (book interviews) | **Roadmap** |
| Submission (client-ready pipeline summary) | **Roadmap** |

> In the room, present "Releasing/Roadmap" confidently as **"shipping this quarter"** —
> the architecture exists; these are sequencing, not unknowns.

## Slide 1 — Context (the German urgency)

**Action title:**
> **In Germany, the constraint on growth isn't demand — it's the 299 days it takes to fill a role.**

**On-slide (three figures, light-blue accent on the numbers):**
- **628,000** open roles unfilled
- **299 days** average time-to-fill
- **7M** worker deficit by 2035

**Layout:** White slide. Three big navy numbers in a row, light-blue underline on each. One grey
caption line at the bottom: *Sources: Federal Employment Agency; OECD; IW.*

**Script:**
> "Let's start with the reality your business already lives every day. Germany has over six
> hundred thousand open roles it cannot fill. The average skilled vacancy now stays open for
> nearly three hundred days — that's ten months of lost productivity per role. And the
> demographics get worse, not better: the IW projects a seven-million-worker deficit by 2035.
> For a staffing agency, this is not a threat — it's the largest demand wave in a generation.
> The only question is whether your team can move fast enough to capture it. Today, most can't —
> and I'll show you exactly where the time goes."

## Slide 2 — Problem (where time & margin leak)

**Action title:**
> **The bottleneck isn't talent — it's that recruiters spend ~80% of the day on admin, not hiring.**

**On-slide (MECE — four leak points, one line each):**
1. **Admin overload** — 17.7 hrs/vacancy on manual tasks
2. **Keyword tools miss good fits** — 88% of execs say qualified candidates get screened out
3. **Your own database goes cold** — ~75% of records never re-engaged
4. **Fragmented systems** — 15–25% margin leakage

**Layout:** White slide, vertical list of 4. Light-blue accent only on the four percentages.
No icons larger than the text.

**Script:**
> "Here's the uncomfortable part: the talent shortage is real, but most of the loss is
> self-inflicted by tooling. Your recruiters spend close to eighty percent of their day on
> admin — formatting CVs, chasing scheduling, copying data between systems. That's almost
> eighteen hours of pure overhead per vacancy. Second, the keyword filters in most ATSs are
> literal — a 'Product Lead' never surfaces for a 'Product Manager' search, which is why
> Harvard found eighty-eight percent of executives admit qualified people get screened out.
> Third — and this one is pure money left on the table — about three-quarters of the candidates
> already sitting in your own database are never looked at again. And finally, because these
> jobs live across disconnected tools, fifteen to twenty-five percent of margin simply leaks
> away. Four leaks. All fixable."

## Slide 4 — Trust (the German compliance moat)

**Action title:**
> **Built to clear the August 2026 EU AI Act bar — and to pass your Betriebsrat.**

**On-slide (four pillars, one line each):**
- **DSGVO / BDSG** — your data stays yours, fully auditable
- **EU AI Act–ready** — hiring AI is *high-risk*; we're built for the 02.08.2026 deadline
- **AGG-fair** — protected attributes removed before scoring; bias monitored
- **Betriebsrat-ready** — every ranking is explainable; a human always decides (§95 BetrVG)

**Layout:** This is the **one dark slide** — Deep Navy background, white text, light-blue accents
on the four pillar names. Signals seriousness.

**Script:**
> "And in Germany, speed without trust is worthless — so we built for the bar that generic AI
> tools quietly fail. Three things. One: data protection. Everything runs under DSGVO and BDSG,
> your data never trains anyone else's model, and every decision is logged. Two: from August
> 2026, the EU AI Act treats hiring AI as high-risk — Recruitr is built to that standard now,
> not retrofitted later. Three, and this is where most vendors get caught: the AI Act alone
> isn't enough in Germany — your Betriebsrat has co-determination rights over selection criteria
> under §95 BetrVG. That's why every Recruitr ranking comes with a plain-language reason, the
> protected attributes are stripped before scoring under the AGG, and a human recruiter always
> makes the final call. Recruitr is the version of this you can actually defend — to your works
> council, your DPO, and the regulator."
